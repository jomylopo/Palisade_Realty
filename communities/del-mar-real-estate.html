Location

Where is Del Mar?

Del Mar sits along one of Southern California's most dramatic stretches of coastline — perched on sandstone bluffs above the Pacific where the San Dieguito River meets the sea, just 20 miles north of Downtown San Diego. With I-5 running east of the village, Del Mar is remarkably accessible while retaining the quiet, insular feel of a true coastal enclave.

The Seaside Village

Blufftop Prestige,
Village Charm

Del Mar is one of San Diego's most exclusive small cities — a charming seaside village of just 4,300 residents perched above dramatic coastal bluffs where the Pacific meets the San Dieguito River Lagoon. With only about 1,700 homes and a strict commitment to low-density living, Del Mar maintains the intimacy, beauty, and exclusivity that larger coastal communities simply cannot replicate. The $2 million median reflects a combination of jaw-dropping ocean views, walkable village life, and an inventory that rarely meets demand.

The city's identity is anchored by two beloved institutions: the Del Mar Racetrack and Fairgrounds — where thoroughbred racing has drawn summer crowds since 1937 under the motto "Where the Turf Meets the Surf" — and the San Diego County Fair, the nation's largest county fair by attendance. Year-round, Del Mar Village's boutique-lined streets, acclaimed restaurants, weekly Farmers Market, and direct beach access create a self-contained world that residents never tire of.

What Makes It Special

Living in Del Mar

Del Mar Racetrack & Fairgrounds

Since 1937, the Del Mar Thoroughbred Club has hosted California's most celebrated racing season under the motto "Where the Turf Meets the Surf." Opening Day is a beloved local tradition with elaborate hats and festive crowds, while the San Diego County Fair brings over 1.5 million visitors each summer.

Dramatic Coastal Bluffs & Beach Access

Del Mar's sandstone bluffs rise above one of San Diego's finest stretches of coastline. The beach at 15th Street is the community gathering point — a wide, sandy shore framed by dramatic cliffs, with calm waters and reliable surf breaks beloved by locals year-round.

Del Mar Village

The village core along Camino Del Mar is a rare coastal walkable district — independent boutiques, acclaimed restaurants, wine bars, and the beloved Del Mar Farmers Market create a genuine sense of place that larger communities lack. Parking is easy; the energy is authentic.

L'Auberge Del Mar Resort

Del Mar's anchor luxury property — the Forbes-rated L'Auberge Resort sits steps from the beach with a world-class spa, farm-to-table dining, and infinity pool overlooking the Pacific. For residents it's a backyard amenity; for visitors it's the reason Del Mar belongs on every California coastal itinerary.

San Dieguito Lagoon & Nature Preserve

The San Dieguito Lagoon State Marine Conservation Area is a protected 558-acre wetland estuary teeming with shorebirds and migratory species. Walking and biking trails along the lagoon provide a natural buffer between Del Mar and the surrounding region — one of the city's most treasured amenities.

Top-Ranked Schools

Del Mar students are served by two highly regarded districts: Del Mar Union School District for K–6, and San Dieguito Union High School District for grades 7–12. Torrey Pines High School is among California's highest-performing public high schools, a consistent draw for families relocating to the area.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Del Mar

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Del Mar real estate.

What is the median home price in Del Mar?
+
The median home price in Del Mar is approximately $2 million as of 2026. Entry-level condos and townhomes start around $1.2M–$1.5M, while single-family homes in Del Mar Heights and Del Mar Village typically range from $1.8M to $4M. Blufftop and oceanfront properties on Coast Boulevard regularly command $5M–$8M+. Del Mar is among the most expensive residential markets in San Diego County. Contact Hedda Parashos for current conditions in specific neighborhoods.
What are the different neighborhoods within Del Mar?
+
Del Mar's primary neighborhoods include: Del Mar Village (walkable village core, boutiques and dining), Coast Boulevard Area (oceanfront and blufftop estates, highest price tier), Del Mar Heights (elevated views, established single-family homes), Del Mar Terrace (flat, walkable, community-oriented), Ocean View Hills (panoramic views, larger lots), Solana Highlands (quiet residential, inland), Del Mar Country Club (gated, golf community, prestige), and Carmel Heights border area (newer construction, value relative to the city).
Is Del Mar a good place for families?
+
Del Mar is outstanding for families. Torrey Pines High School consistently ranks among California's top public high schools for academics, athletics, and college placement. The Del Mar Union School District is also highly regarded at the elementary level. The community offers low crime rates, abundant outdoor recreation, the Del Mar Dog Beach, Farmers Market, and a tight-knit community culture. The racetrack and county fair add a unique festive dimension to the family calendar each summer.
What is the Del Mar Racetrack season?
+
The Del Mar Thoroughbred Club summer meet runs mid-July through early September each year. Opening Day — traditionally the third Wednesday of July — is a beloved San Diego tradition featuring elaborate hats, crowds of 40,000+, and a festive atmosphere unlike any other race meet in America. The fall meet follows in November. The San Diego County Fair occupies the fairgrounds in June each year, drawing 1.5 million visitors and temporarily increasing local traffic and demand for short-term rentals.
What school districts serve Del Mar?
+
Del Mar is served by two separate districts. Del Mar Union School District (DMUSD) covers kindergarten through 6th grade with several strong elementary campuses including Del Mar Hills Academy, Carmel Del Mar, and Sage Canyon. San Dieguito Union High School District (SDUHSD) covers grades 7–12, with Earl Warren Middle School and the highly ranked Torrey Pines High School serving most Del Mar students. School assignments are address-specific — always verify with the districts before purchasing.
How far is Del Mar from downtown San Diego?
+
Del Mar is approximately 20 miles north of downtown San Diego — roughly a 25-minute drive via I-5 in normal traffic. Rush-hour commutes can extend to 40–50 minutes southbound in the morning. The Coaster commuter rail provides a scenic alternative, with service connecting Del Mar (Solana Beach station) to downtown's Santa Fe Depot in about 45 minutes. Many Del Mar residents work in North County tech corridors in Carmel Valley, Sorrento Valley, and Torrey Pines, keeping commutes under 15 minutes.
What makes Del Mar unique among coastal San Diego communities?
+
Del Mar's uniqueness comes from its combination of extreme scarcity (just 4,300 residents and ~1,700 homes), a walkable village core with genuine boutique character, the iconic racetrack tradition, and blufftop geography that limits development while maximizing views. Unlike La Jolla, which has grown substantially, Del Mar has deliberately kept its small-town scale. Unlike Coronado, it's on the mainland with direct freeway access to San Diego's North County employment centers. It is, in effect, an irreplaceable collision of beach town, luxury village, and Southern California heritage.
What are HOA fees like in Del Mar?
+
A significant portion of Del Mar's single-family homes carry no HOA at all — particularly in older Village and Heights neighborhoods. Townhome and planned communities typically run $200–$600/month. Condo complexes range from $400–$900/month. The Del Mar Country Club gated community ranges from $800–$1,500+/month for club access and amenities. Oceanfront luxury buildings can reach $2,500+/month for high-end services. Hedda can help you understand exactly what's included and whether the reserve funds are healthy before you make an offer.
The Del Mar Lifestyle

Village by the Sea,
Beautifully Unhurried

Life in Del Mar moves at the pace of the ocean tides — mornings begin with coffee on a bluff-edge terrace watching the surf, afternoons belong to the beach at 15th Street or a stroll through the village browsing the farmers market, and evenings invariably end at a candlelit table on Camino Del Mar with the salt air drifting in from the Pacific.

Summer transforms the community when racing season arrives — Opening Day at the Del Mar Racetrack is one of San Diego's most celebrated social events, drawing tens of thousands of visitors while longtime residents simply walk down from their homes in their finest hats. The rest of the year belongs to residents who treasure the village's quiet confidence: a luxury resort, a beloved dog beach, a protected lagoon, and a community that has chosen beauty and scarcity over growth.

  • Del Mar Farmers Market — every Saturday morning year-round
  • Del Mar Racetrack Opening Day (July) — San Diego's most festive afternoon
  • San Diego County Fair (June) — 1.5 million visitors at the fairgrounds
  • Sunset at Seagrove Park — the community's nightly ritual
  • Del Mar Dog Beach — off-leash freedom for four-legged residents
  • Coaster Rail Service — scenic train link to downtown San Diego
