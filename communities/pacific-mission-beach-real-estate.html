Location

Where is Pacific & Mission Beach?

Nestled between the Pacific Ocean and Mission Bay on San Diego's central coast, Pacific Beach and Mission Beach form a 4-mile stretch of some of California's most coveted shoreline. The iconic boardwalk, world-class surf, and Mission Bay's 4,600-acre aquatic playground sit just 15 minutes from downtown San Diego and 10 minutes from the airport.

The Boardwalk Communities

Ocean Energy,
Bay Serenity

Pacific Beach and Mission Beach are San Diego's quintessential beach communities — a sun-drenched 4-mile corridor where the Pacific Ocean meets Mission Bay, and the famous boardwalk pulses with skaters, cyclists, surfers, and beachgoers year-round. These are places where California's iconic beach lifestyle isn't a postcard — it's your Tuesday morning commute.

Pacific Beach (PB) offers a vibrant mix of single-family homes, condos, and ocean-view residences spread across neighborhoods ranging from the high-energy Garnet Avenue corridor to the quieter, family-oriented Crown Point and Sail Bay enclaves along Mission Bay. Mission Beach is a narrow barrier island flanked by the ocean to the west and Mission Bay to the east — offering some of San Diego's most dramatic waterfront living with Belmont Park's historic Giant Dipper roller coaster as its landmark.

What Makes It Special

Living in Pacific & Mission Beach

Pacific Beach Boardwalk

The 3.2-mile oceanfront boardwalk is the social heart of PB — a continuous promenade stretching from South Mission Beach to Crystal Pier, alive with cyclists, skaters, volleyballers, and sunset seekers every day of the year. It is as close to California's beach ideal as anywhere on earth.

Mission Bay Park

At 4,600 acres, Mission Bay is the largest aquatic park in the United States — a stunning engineered lagoon offering sailing, kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and miles of shoreline parks. Sail Bay, Crown Point, and Fiesta Island anchor its distinct coves and attract water lovers from across Southern California.

Belmont Park & Historic Pier

Belmont Park's landmark Giant Dipper roller coaster has thrilled visitors since 1925, making it one of California's oldest surviving wooden coasters. Crystal Pier — built in 1927 — offers 66 unique over-water cottage rentals, making it one of the most distinctive lodging experiences on the West Coast.

World-Class Surf & Beach Access

Pacific Beach and Mission Beach offer consistent, beginner-to-intermediate surf breaks that have shaped generations of San Diego surfers. Law Street, Tourmaline Surfing Park, and the breaks at the south end of PB draw a dedicated local surf community while remaining accessible to newcomers learning to paddle out for the first time.

Garnet Avenue Dining & Nightlife

Garnet Avenue is PB's main artery — a lively stretch of chef-driven restaurants, craft beer bars, surf shops, boutiques, and coffee roasters that gives the neighborhood an urban energy. From morning burritos to late-night cocktail bars, Garnet delivers a walkable, social lifestyle unlike any other coastal neighborhood in San Diego.

Investment & Vacation Rental Appeal

Pacific Beach and Mission Beach rank among San Diego County's strongest short-term rental markets, attracting year-round visitors drawn by the boardwalk, weather, and proximity to SeaWorld and the airport. For buyers seeking income-producing property or a second home with rental potential, few zip codes in California outperform 92109.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Pacific & Mission Beach

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Pacific & Mission Beach real estate.

What is the median home price in Pacific & Mission Beach?
+
The median home price in Pacific & Mission Beach is approximately $1.2 million as of 2026. Entry-level condos in South PB and Mission Beach Shores start around $700,000–$900,000. Single-family homes in Crown Point and the Law Street area range from $1M to $2M. Beachfront and bayfront properties — especially on Mission Beach's narrow strip or along Sail Bay — can easily reach $2M–$4M+. Contact Hedda Parashos for current market conditions by specific neighborhood.
What is the difference between Pacific Beach and Mission Beach?
+
Pacific Beach (PB) is the larger northern community — a diverse neighborhood with single-family homes, condos, and apartments spread across a wider area that includes Crown Point along Mission Bay and the Garnet Avenue commercial corridor. Mission Beach is a narrow barrier strip between the ocean and Mission Bay south of PB — denser, more vacation-oriented, and home to Belmont Park. Mission Beach properties are often on narrower lots with higher price-per-square-foot due to their unique location between two bodies of water.
Are short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO) allowed in this area?
+
San Diego has a regulated short-term rental (STR) program. As of 2024, the city issues licenses for whole-home STRs on a mission-tiered basis — with Mission Beach receiving a dedicated allocation due to its historic vacation rental character. Licensing is limited and competitive, and HOAs may impose additional restrictions. Buyers interested in rental income should work with Hedda to verify a specific property's STR eligibility and review the current city licensing waitlist status before purchasing.
Is Pacific Beach a good place for families?
+
Pacific Beach is increasingly popular with families — particularly the Crown Point, Sail Bay, and North PB enclaves, which offer quieter streets, access to Mission Bay parks, and a more residential character. Crown Point Elementary and Kate Sessions Elementary are both well-regarded. The proximity to Kate Sessions Memorial Park, Mission Bay's recreational amenities, and excellent beach access make it a genuinely appealing environment for families. South PB and Mission Beach are livelier and more suited to young professionals or investor buyers.
What are HOA fees like in Pacific & Mission Beach?
+
Single-family homes in Pacific Beach typically carry no HOA. Condo complexes range from $350–$700/month for standard complexes and $500–$1,100+/month for beachfront or bayfront properties with amenities. Mission Beach condos tend to run higher due to the intensive maintenance demands of coastal salt air exposure. Always review the reserve fund health and any pending special assessments — Hedda can help you interpret the HOA financials before you make an offer.
How far is Pacific Beach from downtown San Diego?
+
Pacific Beach is approximately 7–9 miles from downtown San Diego — about 15 minutes via I-8, and 10 minutes from San Diego International Airport via the I-5/I-8 interchange. Traffic on I-8 heading eastbound during rush hour can extend commutes, but the overall location is remarkably central for a beach community of this quality. Mission Bay Drive also offers a scenic alternative route to the airport and Point Loma.
What ZIP code covers Pacific Beach and Mission Beach?
+
Both Pacific Beach and Mission Beach fall within ZIP code 92109 — one of San Diego County's most recognized coastal ZIP codes. This single ZIP encompasses the full range of neighborhoods from North Pacific Beach and Crown Point to the Mission Beach boardwalk strip and Sail Bay waterfront. When searching for homes, filtering by 92109 captures the complete area inventory.
What property types are most common in Pacific & Mission Beach?
+
The market is a mix of condos and attached units (most common at ~55%), single-family homes (strong supply in North PB, Crown Point, and the Law Street area), and a small number of multi-family income properties. Mission Beach skews heavily toward condos and townhomes given its narrow lot widths. Detached single-family homes in Pacific Beach range from 1920s beach cottages to newer 3-story contemporary builds — and when they come to market with views, they move quickly.
The Pacific Beach Lifestyle

Sun, Bay, &
Boardwalk Living

Life in Pacific and Mission Beach moves to the rhythm of the tides. Mornings start with a sunrise surf session or an oceanfront run along the boardwalk; afternoons belong to Mission Bay's kayaks and paddleboards; evenings wind down at a rooftop bar on Garnet Avenue or with a meal at one of the dozen restaurants walkable from almost any address in the neighborhood.

This is California beach culture at its most authentic and most accessible — without the pretension of La Jolla or the sprawl of a resort town. The community skews young and active, but Crown Point and Sail Bay are drawing a growing wave of families who want walkability, bay access, and good schools without sacrificing the energy that makes San Diego's coastline world-famous.
