Where is Point Loma?

A sweeping peninsula stretching south into the Pacific — Point Loma is bordered by San Diego Bay to the east and the open ocean to the west, offering a rare combination of harbor calm, dramatic cliffs, and coastal village character just minutes from downtown.

The Coastal Peninsula

Harbor Views,
Coastal Character

Point Loma is one of San Diego's most beloved communities — a sun-soaked peninsula where Craftsman bungalows and mid-century ranches meet panoramic harbor views, dramatic ocean bluffs, and a genuine neighborhood spirit that's increasingly rare in Southern California. From Sunset Cliffs Natural Park to the revitalized Liberty Station arts district, Point Loma rewards those who value both natural beauty and community character.

The area's military heritage runs deep — Naval Base Point Loma anchors the southern tip — giving the community a strong sense of pride, stability, and civic identity. Cabrillo National Monument crowns the peninsula with 360-degree views that rank among the finest in California, while Shelter Island's marinas and waterfront restaurants deliver a resort-like atmosphere on a Tuesday afternoon.

What Makes It Special

Living in Point Loma

Cabrillo National Monument

The crown of the peninsula — a 160-acre national monument with 360-degree views of San Diego Bay, the Pacific Ocean, Mexico, and on clear days, the Channel Islands. The tide pools and whale-watching overlooks are world-class.

Sunset Cliffs Natural Park

68 acres of dramatic sandstone bluffs along the Pacific — a beloved gathering spot for surfers, tide-poolers, and sunset seekers. The cliffside paths and sea caves are among the most photographed vistas in San Diego.

Liberty Station Arts District

A 361-acre transformation of the former Naval Training Center into San Diego's premier arts, dining, and community hub. Galleries, chef-driven restaurants, a public market, fitness studios, and weekend events draw visitors from across the county.

Shelter Island Marina Living

San Diego's premier waterfront destination — Shelter Island's marinas, yacht clubs, resort hotels, and waterfront restaurants deliver a permanent vacation atmosphere. Bayside sunsets here rival anything in the Mediterranean.

Naval Heritage & Community Pride

Home to Naval Base Point Loma and Naval Medical Center, the area has a strong military community that contributes to its well-maintained neighborhoods, civic engagement, and stable property values year over year.

Ocean Beach Village Spirit

OB — as locals call it — is Point Loma's eclectic beach-town neighbor. The legendary Wednesday farmers market, Newport Avenue's antique shops, the OB Pier, and a fiercely independent local culture give this enclave an energy found nowhere else in San Diego.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Point Loma

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Point Loma real estate.

What is the median home price in Point Loma?
+
The median home price in Point Loma is approximately $1.1 million as of 2026. Entry-level condos in Ocean Beach and Liberty Station start around $700,000–$850,000, while single-family homes in Loma Portal and Point Loma Heights typically range from $900,000 to $1.6M. Bayfront and view properties in La Playa or Sunset Cliffs can easily reach $2M–$4M+. Contact Hedda Parashos for current market conditions in specific neighborhoods.
What are the different neighborhoods within Point Loma?
+
Point Loma encompasses several distinct enclaves: Point Loma Heights (central, harbor views), La Playa (bayfront estates, yacht access), Shelter Island (marina district, resort atmosphere), Liberty Station (arts and dining district in former NTC), Loma Portal (family-friendly, tree-lined blocks), Ocean Beach (eclectic beach town, OB Pier), Sunset Cliffs (dramatic ocean bluffs), and the Wooded Area (mature residential, established streets).
Is Point Loma a good place for families?
+
Point Loma is consistently rated one of San Diego's best neighborhoods for families. Point Loma High School has strong academics and athletic programs. The area offers low crime rates, walkable streets in Loma Portal and the Heights, abundant outdoor activities, and a strong community identity. Liberty Station's parks and events calendar make it particularly family-friendly, and the proximity to Cabrillo National Monument provides exceptional outdoor recreation without a long drive.
What ZIP codes cover Point Loma?
+
Point Loma is primarily covered by two ZIP codes: 92106 (southern Point Loma — La Playa, Shelter Island, Cabrillo area, Liberty Station) and 92107 (northern Point Loma — Ocean Beach, Sunset Cliffs, Point Loma Heights, Loma Portal). When searching for homes, both ZIPs should be included to capture the full range of available inventory.
What is Liberty Station and why does it matter for buyers?
+
Liberty Station is the redevelopment of the former Naval Training Center — 361 acres of historic brick buildings transformed into San Diego's leading arts, dining, and community district. For homebuyers, it means walkable access to 30+ restaurants, galleries, the Liberty Public Market, fitness studios, and a beautifully maintained waterfront park. Properties near Liberty Station have appreciated significantly as the district has matured, and new residential development continues to attract buyers.
How far is Point Loma from downtown San Diego?
+
Point Loma is approximately 5–7 miles from downtown San Diego — a 10–15 minute drive via Rosecrans Street or the I-8/I-5 corridor. Traffic is generally manageable outside of rush hours. SAN Airport is also about 15 minutes away via Harbor Drive, making Point Loma one of the most conveniently located coastal neighborhoods in the county.
Are there single-family homes available in Point Loma?
+
Yes — unlike Downtown San Diego, Point Loma has strong single-family home inventory. Loma Portal, Point Loma Heights, the Wooded Area, and Ocean Beach all offer detached homes on standard lots. La Playa and Sunset Cliffs have larger estate-style properties. Liberty Station and Shelter Island feature more condo and townhome product. Overall, roughly 48% of residents are homeowners, reflecting the area's robust SFR market.
What are HOA fees like in Point Loma?
+
Many single-family homes in Point Loma have no HOA at all. Townhomes and planned communities typically run $150–$450/month. Condo complexes range from $300–$650/month. Waterfront properties near Shelter Island can reach $1,200+/month for luxury amenities. Liberty Station condos generally fall in the $300–$550/month range. Always review HOA financials and reserve fund health before purchasing — Hedda can help you interpret the documents.
The Point Loma Lifestyle

Peninsula Living,
Beautifully Balanced

Life in Point Loma moves at its own unhurried pace — mornings start with coffee on a porch overlooking the harbor, afternoons belong to the beach or the cliffs at Sunset Cliffs Natural Park, and evenings wind down at a waterfront table on Shelter Island with the downtown skyline glowing across the bay.

The community is tightly knit and fiercely proud — the kind of place where neighbors know each other's names and fourth-generation families still live on the same block. It's cosmopolitan enough to offer world-class dining at Liberty Station, independent enough to resist the forces that have homogenized other San Diego neighborhoods.

  • OB Farmers Market — every Wednesday evening year-round
  • Cabrillo Festival (September) — historic reenactment & harbor events
  • Ocean Beach Street Fair & Chili Cook-Off (June)
  • Fleet Week San Diego (October) — ship tours & air shows
  • Liberty Station Holiday Village (December)
