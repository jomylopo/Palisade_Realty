Location

Where is Encinitas?

Encinitas sits along six miles of Pacific coastline in North San Diego County — roughly halfway between downtown San Diego and Orange County. Framed by the Pacific Ocean to the west and the rolling hills of inland San Diego to the east, the city blends coastal accessibility with genuine small-town character, just 35 minutes from the city center via I-5.

The Surf Town

Authentic Coast,
North County Soul

Encinitas is North San Diego County's most celebrated coastal city — a place where the pace of life slows to match the rhythm of the surf, and where five distinct communities each carry their own unmistakable identity. From the artist galleries and bohemian coffee shops lining Leucadia's stretch of Coast Highway 101, to the equestrian estates of Olivenhain and the world-famous right-point break at Swami's, Encinitas rewards buyers who want something more authentic than a master-planned suburb.

Incorporated in 1986 from the five communities of Old Encinitas, New Encinitas, Cardiff-by-the-Sea, Leucadia, and Olivenhain, the city has carefully preserved its agricultural roots and independent character. Flower fields still dot the hillsides — Encinitas is historically the poinsettia capital of the United States — and the Self-Realization Fellowship's gold lotus towers overlooking Swami's remain one of the most iconic landmarks on California's coast.

What Makes It Special

Living in Encinitas

World-Class Surfing at Swami's

Swami's Beach hosts one of California's great right-point breaks — a long, peeling wave that has shaped Encinitas's soul for generations. The cliffside park above the break overlooks the Self-Realization Fellowship gardens, creating one of the most spiritually charged landscapes on the California coast.

Self-Realization Fellowship

The iconic gold lotus towers of the Self-Realization Fellowship Hermitage mark the Encinitas skyline and draw visitors from around the world. The oceanfront meditation gardens — open to the public — rank among the most serene and beautiful spaces in all of Southern California.

Historic Coast Highway 101

The original Coast Highway 101 runs through the heart of Encinitas, lined with surf shops, farm-to-table restaurants, yoga studios, and local plant nurseries that have defined the city's character for decades. Leucadia's stretch is particularly beloved — a bohemian corridor unlike anything else in North County.

San Elijo Lagoon Nature Reserve

One of San Diego County's largest coastal wetlands, San Elijo Lagoon spans over 1,000 acres on the Encinitas–Solana Beach border. Seven miles of trails wind through rare coastal sage scrub and riparian habitat, supporting over 300 bird species — a world-class destination for birders and nature lovers.

Award-Winning Schools

Encinitas is served by two highly regarded school districts — Encinitas Union Elementary School District and San Dieguito Union High School District. La Costa Canyon High School and San Dieguito High School Academy consistently earn top ratings, attracting families who prioritize educational quality as much as coastal lifestyle.

Olivenhain's Rural Prestige

Encinitas's easternmost community, Olivenhain, delivers an entirely different lifestyle — gated estates, equestrian properties, and rolling hillsides that feel a world removed from the coast just five miles away. With lot sizes from one to ten acres and homes regularly exceeding $3M, Olivenhain is among the most exclusive addresses in North County.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About Encinitas

The questions buyers ask most when exploring Encinitas real estate.

What is the median home price in Encinitas?
+
The median home price in Encinitas is approximately $1.4 million as of 2026. Entry-level condos and townhomes in New Encinitas and along El Camino Real start around $800,000–$1.1M, while single-family homes in Old Encinitas and Leucadia typically range from $1.2M to $3.5M. Cardiff-by-the-Sea beachfront homes and Olivenhain estates regularly exceed $3M–$5M+. Contact Hedda Parashos for current market conditions in specific neighborhoods.
What are the different communities within Encinitas?
+
The City of Encinitas was formed in 1986 from five distinct communities: Old Encinitas (the original surf town along Coast Highway 101), New Encinitas (planned residential east of I-5), Cardiff-by-the-Sea (laid-back surfing community at the south end), Leucadia (bohemian arts enclave at the north end), and Olivenhain (rural equestrian community in the eastern hills). Each retains its own identity, price point, and lifestyle character.
Is Encinitas a good place for families?
+
Encinitas is consistently rated one of the best places to raise a family in San Diego County. The Encinitas Union Elementary School District and San Dieguito Union High School District both rank in the top tier statewide. The city has low crime rates, excellent parks, and a strong community identity. New Encinitas and Olivenhain are particularly popular with families for their larger lot sizes, good schools, and quieter residential character.
What ZIP codes cover Encinitas?
+
Encinitas is primarily covered by ZIP code 92024, which encompasses the entire city including Old Encinitas, New Encinitas, Leucadia, and Olivenhain. Cardiff-by-the-Sea uses 92007 as its primary ZIP. When searching for homes in the broader Encinitas area, including both ZIPs will capture the full range of available inventory.
What is the surf culture like in Encinitas?
+
Surfing is central to Encinitas's identity. Swami's — named for the Self-Realization Fellowship above the break — is one of California's most revered right-point waves and a State Historic Surfing Monument. Cardiff Reef, Grandview, and Moonlight Beach offer breaks suited to all skill levels. The local surf scene is tight-knit and proud, with deep roots that have shaped the city's politics, architecture, and character for over 60 years. Encinitas hosts events like the Swami's Invitational and the Cardiff Kook Run that celebrate this heritage.
How far is Encinitas from downtown San Diego?
+
Encinitas is approximately 25–28 miles north of downtown San Diego — roughly a 30–40 minute drive via I-5 South, depending on traffic. The Coaster commuter rail also provides service between Encinitas and downtown San Diego, making Encinitas a viable choice for professionals who work in the city but prefer a quieter coastal lifestyle. The Encinitas Coaster station connects to the San Diego trolley system.
What types of homes are available in Encinitas?
+
Encinitas offers a diverse housing stock spanning all four major property types. Single-family detached homes dominate west of I-5 in Old Encinitas and Leucadia, where Craftsman bungalows, Cape Cods, and modern coastal builds sit on lots from 4,000 to 10,000+ sq ft. New Encinitas offers newer planned-community homes and townhomes. Cardiff has oceanview estates and beachfront cottages. Olivenhain features rural ranchettes and equestrian properties on large acreage. Condos and townhomes are concentrated near El Camino Real and the I-5 corridor.
What are HOA fees like in Encinitas?
+
Many single-family homes in Old Encinitas and Leucadia have no HOA at all — these freestanding neighborhoods were developed organically over decades without planned community associations. New Encinitas planned communities typically run $150–$500/month. Condo complexes range from $250–$600/month. Gated Olivenhain communities can range from $250–$800+/month. Oceanfront and premium view properties in Cardiff or Seaside may reach $1,500+/month for luxury amenities. Always review HOA financials before purchasing — Hedda can help you interpret the documents.
The Encinitas Lifestyle

Barefoot Living,
Deeply Rooted

Life in Encinitas moves at the pace of the surf report. Mornings begin with a paddle out at Swami's or a walk through the Self-Realization Fellowship's cliff gardens above the break. Afternoons belong to the San Elijo Lagoon trails, the plant nurseries that line Old Highway 101, or a late lunch at one of Cardiff's legendary reef-view restaurants.

The community carries a fiercely independent spirit — Encinitas has repeatedly voted to preserve its small-town character against development pressure, and locals take pride in the city's unique blend of surf culture, agricultural heritage, and spiritual tradition. It's the kind of place where the same families have lived for three generations and where new residents quickly become regulars.

  • Leucadia Farmers Market — Sundays year-round on Paul Ecke Central fields
  • Encinitas Street Fair & Chili Cook-Off (June)
  • Cardiff Kook Run (December) — costumed 10K around the Cardiff Kook statue
  • Moonlight Amphitheatre Summer Concert Series
  • San Elijo Lagoon Bird Count (winter) — community science event
